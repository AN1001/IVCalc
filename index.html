IV Calc

Welcome
IV Calc is a powerful investment platform calculator
Investment can be tricky at time due to high levels of uncertainty alongside other complex factors. One such factor may be choosing the right platform to hold your investments on, as their charges vary greatly and are often confusing. It's impossible to know with certainty how to get the most out of your money, but using tools like IV Calc it is possible to reach an informed decision.
With IV Calc you can not only compare different investment platforms, but also simulate their use in real time. This is effective against 27 different platforms for both funds and shares.
Begin
To begin simply select your options on the left sidebar, ensure that all fields are filled in with valid values. Once filled in, scroll down and click the 'Run Comparison' button to allow the algorithm to begin.
You may select multiple wrappers at the same time and switch between them in a later menu, please use the (?) button in the top right of a block for more info.

Wrappers
ISA
Funds held within a stocks & shares Individual Savings Account.
Junior ISA
Junior ISA, completely tax-free and cannot be withdrawn from until 18
Direct
Funds held directly, excluding of any tax wrappers.
SIPP
Funds held within a Self-Invested Personal Pension.
Excludes
Does not include ETFs or Investment trusts, these may be accounted for in the 'Shares Investment' tab
Number Of Funds
Number of diffent types of funds held, your monthly addition will be evenly added and a purchase sent out for each one. The commission for this will be calculated and displayed
Trades Per Year
NOTE that Trades Per Year is the total number of trades across all funds held per year
Additionally buying and selling the same stock counts as 2 trades
Excludes
Excludes cost of initially buying the stocks
Trades Per Year
NOTE that Trades Per Year is the total number of trades across all shares held per year
Additionally buying and selling the same stock counts as 2 trades
Comparison Duration
How long the simulation should last for, note that long term simulations may be innacurate as cannot account for change in commissions
Annual Return
The percentage you expect your portfolio to grow by yearly, 7% is an industy average for ETF growth
Proportion Divident Paying Stocks
The percentage of all the stocks you own that pay divident, this is necessary as some brokers charge extra to reinvest divident
